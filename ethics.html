The "Thin Black and Brown Lines" Initiative

Solomon Kin, LLC recognizes that generative AI technologies pose a disproportionate risk to the intellectual property of Black and Brown creators in urban innovation hubs like **Philadelphia** and **Minneapolis**.

This initiative ensures that the NMMMB Protocol is accessible as a tool for Digital Sovereignty, preventing the erasure of cultural contributions through autonomous model drift.

I. Human-Centricity

We refuse to certify any asset that does not demonstrate a "Substantial Human Spark." Our Bureau exists to serve the creator, not the model provider.

II. Algorithmic Justice

We actively audit for "Neural Extraction"—the process where models utilize marginalized cultural aesthetics without transformative human directorship.

III. Regional Custodianship

By maintaining nodes in Philadelphia and Minneapolis, we provide localized forensic support to communities traditionally underserved by the IP legal system.

IV. Transparent Metrology

Our measurement standards (NMMMB) are open to public inspection in the AIRTLR, ensuring that the "Pass/Fail" metrics are never a "Black Box."
